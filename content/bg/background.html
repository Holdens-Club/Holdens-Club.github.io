Background Info

Warning

The background information pages on this website (Background Info and Plot) don't use textual evidence since they just give you background information about the book. Text-based evidence is used on all the main content pages.

The Catcher in the Rye was written by J.D. Salinger and published it 1951. It is J.D. Salinger's only complete novel. The Catcher in the Rye is a story told from the perspective of 16-year old Holden Caulfield, the main character. Holden is generally a cynical, lonely, and rebellious character, which will be explained more and proved in the Characters page. Holden lives in New York City, which is where most of the events in the story take place. The early parts of the story are set in Pencey Prep, Holden's prep school in Pennsylvania. The book begins with Holden in some kind of mental hospital or institution. In the book, Holden essentially narrates the story of his decline into depression and a mental breakdown. The events he narrates in the book happened a year ago around Christmas time.

Upon release, The Catcher in the Rye resonated with many (especially younger) people. The 1950s was a time period where children were expected to conform to social norms, and many felt this undermined their individuality. Holden's constant criticism of social norms in the book and his strong sense of individuality/being true to himself resonated with these people. This is a reason why the book was an instant hit upon release.

However, this book was and still can be controversial. For instance, the book explores teenage sexuality through characters like Stradlater and Holden himself. One chapter even involves Holden and a prostitute in his hotel room. Holden also uses slang and profanity constantly throughout the book. These aspects of the book can offend people and raise concerns when children are reading it. Also, the very informal tone of the book was different from most literature at the time which was formal and dignified. This led to people not taking the book seriously or finding it offensive. Many organizations have banned the book because of these concerns.

Generally, The Catcher in the Rye has a slow plot without much action. Despite this, it can stil be interesting to read and analyze. To move onto our plot overview, you can click here.
